John Von Neumann

Fanpage Introduction

John von Neumann (/vɒn ˈnɔɪmən/; Hungarian: Neumann János Lajos, (December 28, 1903 – February 8, 1957) was a Hungarian-American mathematician, physicist, computer scientist, engineer and polymath. He was regarded as having perhaps the widest coverage of any mathematician of his time[2] and was said to have been "the last representative of the great mathematicians who were equally at home in both pure and applied mathematics".[3][4] He integrated pure and applied sciences.
